1.7 -There are certain guidelines that can cause roadblocks for users implicated in digital projects. The EU GDPR is an act that is designed to protect the data of individuals as of 25/05/2018. It is designed to protect the privacy of individuals, with aims such as: data portability, the right to be forgotten, the right to prevent profiling and the right the rectification and erasure. In a similar way to government bodies, documents involved in projects collect information on individuals. If the personal information is collected electronically then GDPR applies, so the document will need to be ready to be searched. The software used in projects can also implicate GDPR, as many projects involve the enhancement software. This process involves the use of test data, which in turn will involve using personal data. GDPR will ensure that the individual will have the guidelines displayed to them at the start of the test. To help the GDPR in this process, it is good practice for projects to encrypt data produced from tests.
